# Due Diligence Report: NVIDIA Corporation

**Report Date:** April 7, 2026 | **NASDAQ: NVDA**

---

## Executive Summary

NVIDIA has transformed from a gaming GPU maker into the **definitive AI infrastructure provider** of the modern era. FY2026 results are staggering: $215.9B in revenue, $120.1B in net income, and a ~56% net margin — among the highest ever recorded for a company at scale. Its market cap has exceeded $5 trillion, earned through three compounding, structural moats.

The core thesis rests on: (1) proprietary Blackwell GPU architecture, (2) the CUDA software ecosystem with millions of developers and near-prohibitive switching costs, and (3) a vertically integrated AI systems stack (DGX, NVLink, NIM microservices) that customers cannot easily abandon. Key risks — export controls, hyperscaler custom silicon programs, and customer concentration in ~5 accounts — are real but do not represent imminent structural threats.

**Defensibility Score: 10 / 10** — Three compounding moat layers:

1. **Hardware Architecture:** 30+ years of GPU iteration creates a 2–3 generation performance lead. Competitors close the prior-gen gap as NVIDIA ships the next.
2. **CUDA Ecosystem:** $1B+ investment over 15+ years. Millions of developers, hundreds of thousands of CUDA-optimized libraries and models. Switching cost is prohibitive at scale.
3. **Vertical Systems Integration:** NVIDIA sells an AI factory (DGX + NVLink + NIM + cloud APIs) — not just chips. Customers are locked in at infrastructure, software, and organizational levels simultaneously.

**IP:** Extensive patent portfolio across GPU architecture, parallel computing, and AI acceleration. CUDA protected by patents, trade secrets, and ecosystem lock-in. ARM licensing dependency is watch-worthy following the failed $40B acquisition (blocked by regulators, 2022).
