# Due Diligence Report: Stripe

**Prepared:** April 7, 2026
**Classification:** Confidential — Investment Due Diligence

---

## Executive Summary

Stripe has established itself as the dominant infrastructure layer for internet commerce, processing **$1.9 trillion in total payment volume in 2025** — equivalent to 1.6% of global GDP — and generating **$5.1 billion in revenue** while remaining robustly profitable. Founded in 2010 by brothers Patrick and John Collison, the company has grown from a developer-friendly payments API into a full-spectrum financial operating system serving over 5 million businesses, including 90% of the Dow Jones Industrial Average and 80% of the Nasdaq 100. Its February 2026 tender offer at a **$159 billion valuation** signals continued investor conviction and provides employee liquidity ahead of a potential public offering.

The investment thesis rests on three compounding advantages: a 15-year data moat from trillions in processed transactions that makes its fraud detection (Radar) nearly impossible to replicate; a developer ecosystem with extreme switching costs embedded deep within the world's most valuable corporations; and first-mover positioning at the intersection of AI and payments through its Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP), co-developed with OpenAI. The 2025 acquisition trilogy — Bridge ($1.1B), Privy, and Tempo — gives Stripe proprietary stablecoin and blockchain infrastructure that competitors would require years to rebuild.

Key risks include regulatory exposure across 160+ countries, ongoing IPO timing uncertainty, competitive pressure from PayPal, Adyen, and Big Tech entrants, and macroeconomic sensitivity given that revenue is partially volume-based. Nonetheless, Stripe's combination of scale, profitability, technical depth, and category-defining positioning in agentic commerce makes it one of the most compelling late-stage private technology assets available. **Overall assessment: Strong opportunity with manageable risk profile.**

### Mission and Core Business

Stripe's stated mission is to *increase the GDP of the internet*. Its core product suite has evolved far beyond payment processing into a complete financial infrastructure stack:

- **Stripe Payments** — Core payment acceptance across cards, bank transfers, wallets, and local payment methods
- **Stripe Billing** — Subscription and recurring revenue management
- **Stripe Connect** — Marketplace and platform payment orchestration
- **Stripe Radar** — ML-powered fraud detection and prevention
- **Stripe Issuing** — Corporate card and virtual card issuance
- **Stripe Treasury** — Embedded banking and financial accounts
- **Stripe Tax** — Automated global tax calculation and compliance
- **Stripe Capital** — Data-driven merchant cash advances
- **Stripe Identity** — Online identity verification

The **Revenue Suite** (non-payments products) has reached a **$1 billion annual run rate**, demonstrating successful expansion beyond core payment processing and reducing revenue concentration risk.

### Stripe's Competitive Advantages

1. **Developer Brand Supremacy** — 15 years of best-in-class documentation, SDKs, and tooling; a self-reinforcing adoption flywheel
2. **ML Data Moat** — $1.9T annual volume generates fraud training data that compounds annually and is insurmountable for new entrants
3. **Product Breadth** — No competitor offers a comparable full-stack financial OS; each additional product deepens switching costs
4. **Enterprise Entrenchment** — 90% DJIA / 80% Nasdaq 100 coverage; migration is a multi-year, high-risk engineering project
5. **Agentic Commerce First-Mover** — ACP, co-developed with OpenAI, is the leading candidate to become the default payment protocol for AI agents
6. **Proprietary Stablecoin Stack** — Bridge + Privy + Tempo gives Stripe end-to-end stablecoin infrastructure; no payments competitor is close

## Risk Assessment

### Risk Matrix

| Risk | Severity | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regulatory action / licensing revocation | **Critical** | Low–Medium | Extensive compliance (SOC 2, PCI DSS, GDPR, FinCEN); legal teams in all major markets |

| Data breach / security incident | **Critical** | Low | PCI DSS, SOC 2 Type II; significant security investment |

### Most Significant Risk: Regulatory

Operating across 160+ countries and 135+ currencies creates a permanently complex compliance surface. A restrictive ruling in the EU or US could materially impact TPV. However, Stripe's scale, active policy engagement, and compliance infrastructure make adverse surprise less likely than for smaller peers.

## Conclusion

### Key Findings Summary

**Strengths:**
- $1.9T TPV, 34% YoY growth, and confirmed profitability — financial fundamentals are exceptional
- 15-year ML data moat and 90% DJIA / 80% Nasdaq 100 penetration creates structural entrenchment
- ACP + Bridge/Privy/Tempo positions Stripe at the nexus of AI and stablecoin payment rails
- Founder-led by technically credible, long-tenured operators with demonstrated long-term orientation
- Revenue Suite at $1B run rate proves successful expansion beyond core payments

**Risks:**
- Regulatory complexity across 160+ jurisdictions is the most consequential ongoing concern
- IPO timing uncertainty introduces liquidity risk for investors requiring near-term exits
- Take-rate-based revenue model has inherent macroeconomic sensitivity

### Investment Recommendation

Stripe represents a **high-conviction opportunity** for investors with appropriate time horizons. Business quality is not in question — the primary variables are liquidity timing and regulatory tail risk. For long-term capital, the combination of dominant market position, confirmed profitability, and emerging-category optionality in agentic commerce and stablecoins supports a compelling risk-adjusted case.
